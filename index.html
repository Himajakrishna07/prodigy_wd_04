Projects

Project 1

Title: Solar powered aquaponics
Duration: 1 months
Project Description:
Designed and implemented a solar-powered aquaponics system, integrating aquaculture and hydroponics to create a sustainable farming solution
The system utilizes solar energy to power pumps, filters, and other components,
creating a closed-loop environment where fish waste fertilizes plant growth, and plants filter and purify water for fish.
Achieved optimal resource efficiency, reduced environmental footprint, and ensured year-round production of fresh produce.
Key Contributions:
• Designed system layout and selected components for optimal efficiency and performance.
• Installed and configured solar panels, water pumps, biofilters, and monitoring systems.
• Conducted regular monitoring and maintenance to ensure system integrity and functionality.
• Collaborated with team members to troubleshoot issues and optimize system performance.
• Presented project findings and outcomes to stakeholders, demonstrating the viability and benefits of solar-powered aquaponics for sustainable agriculture.
Impact:
• Demonstrated the feasibility and effectiveness of solar-powered aquaponics as a sustainable farming solution.
• Raised awareness of sustainable agricultural practices and contributed to environmental conservation efforts.
• Provided a practical example of integrating renewable energy with agricultural innovation for a more resilient food system.

Project 2

Title : Automated plant watering system using Arduino uno
Duration: 1 week
Project Description :
Developed and implemented an automatic plant watering system using Arduino Uno microcontroller.
Designed to provide precise and customizable irrigation to potted plants,
the system incorporates moisture sensors to detect soil moisture levels and activates a water pump accordingly.
Utilized Arduino programming language to create algorithms for sensor readings and pump control,
ensuring efficient water usage and plant health.
Key Contributions:
• Designed system architecture and selected components based on project requirements.
• Programmed Arduino Uno microcontroller to interface with moisture sensors and control water pump operation.
• Conducted rigorous testing and calibration to optimize system performance and reliability.
• Implemented user-friendly interface for adjusting watering schedules and sensor thresholds.
• Documented project specifications, codebase, and assembly instructions for future reference and replication.
Impact:
• Provided an innovative solution for automated plant care, catering to individuals with busy schedules or limited access to water.
• Demonstrated proficiency in hardware prototyping, sensor integration, and microcontroller programming.
• Enhanced understanding of electronics, automation, and software development through hands-on project experience.
• Contributed to environmental sustainability by promoting efficient water usage and supporting plant growth and well-being.
